## Phase IV Individual Contributions

### Backend contributions
At the launch of phase four most of the backend ML\DS stuff was in place, the only thing we needed to do was add our csvs into the database. I updated the schemas and got that taken care of.

### Frontend contributions
So with the user testing and feedback on phase 3 we had our work cut out for us. I focused on the EU official persona first. I built out the dashboard, lesson approval page with all its features, and the voter turnout admin page.  My contributions here made sure that the eu offical could meet all of the requirements we set out for it. I also did a ton of work on the teacher persona. I created the my students page and both of the tabs on it, the analytics page, and most of the lesson page. For lessons specifically, I added the create lesson functionality and wired it end-to-end so that when a teacher submits a lesson it shows up immediately in the EU official's pending lessons list. I also made the tables for the teacher persona static after Bobby tore them apart during the user testing. I wasn't too involved with the student persona, all I did for that was add the feature comparison table and heatmap to the country simulations. 

### Teamwork
I just wanted to compliment my teammates, all four of us worked so hard on this and I'm really proud of what we've acomplished here. Couldn't have asked for a better group

## Fun exploring the Trip

Strausburg and Luxembourg were both great to visit even with the whole hotel situation. I was able to check out some really cool stuff, there was a military museum close by that was interesting and we got out to a giant castle overlooking the region which was sick. I also got to try some of the pasteries which were delicious. I'm glad we made the detour, it was a lovely little town. I was able to get out to Antwerp last weekend and it was a cool place, it felt very technological and rich. Stepping out the train station and seeing a bunch of diamond shops was nuts. The C-Mine expedition was an excellent expedition too, it was a lot of fun even if it was in the middle of nowhere. I actually have some photos this time too. On a serious note this whole dialouge has been a wonderful experience and I'm incredibly grateful that I got this opportunity.
