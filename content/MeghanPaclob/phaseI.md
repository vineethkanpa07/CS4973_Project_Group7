# The Beguinage

I enjoyed the tour of Leuven on the second day. I'm personally interested in looking and learning about architecture and the many different styles. I found it really interesting that most of the city buildings were burned down and rebuilt to be match their original style rather than a more "modern" style for the time. 

While it was interesting to learn about the history of the different hubs of the city, the highlight for me was visiting and learning about the Beguinage. A Beguinage is an all-women community that didn't allow men in the gates past 7 pm, and many of the women had jobs like brewing beer or baking bread. There definitely seemed like a lot of benefits to living in Beguinage, so if it were a more widespread and current practice I definitely think I would live in one or at least experience living in one for some time.
